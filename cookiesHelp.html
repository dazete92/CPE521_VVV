What is a Cookie? (click)
A cookie is a piece of data sent from a website that is stored in the browser.

What are cookies used for (click)
Cookies are used to remember browser state information (browsing activity, passwords, etc.). Each time a website is loaded, the browser sends the cookie back to the website to remind it of previous activity. One popular example that uses cookies is an online shopping cart, where items remain in the cart when the online store is revisited.

Security Implications of Cookies (click)
If cookies aren't secured properly or stolen, they can be used by an attacker to steal information or impersonate a valid user.

Best Practices (click)
Cookies should be encrypted or encoded to protect their information. The use of multiple, smaller cookies versus one large cookie can also be beneficial
